# Induction-Program-Report
My Induction Program Report 
## **Day 1 – Welcome & Orientation**

The first day of the Induction Program began with great energy and excitement as all the new students gathered to mark the beginning of their academic journey at **Guru Nanak Dev Engineering College**.

The day started with a **prayer at Gurudwara Sahib** from **8:30 AM to 9:30 AM**. The peaceful atmosphere and the spiritual start filled everyone with positivity and motivation for the days ahead.

Following the prayer, we had an inspiring session with **Shri Ashok Singla**, Financial Advisor, who delivered a motivational address to the students. He encouraged us to stay focused on our goals, work hard with dedication, and take full advantage of the opportunities that college life offers.

Later, we were taken on a **campus tour**, where we explored the various departments, laboratories, library, sports facilities, and other important areas of the college. This helped us become familiar with our new surroundings and understand the resources available to us.

Overall, the first day set a positive tone for the entire program. The combination of spiritual guidance, motivational words, and an introduction to the campus created a welcoming environment for all the students.
## **Day 2 – Skill Assessment & Wellness Sessions**

The second day of the Induction Program began with an **English Proficiency Test** from **9:30 AM to 10:30 AM**, designed to assess our current language skills and help identify areas for improvement.  
This was followed by a **Mathematics Proficiency Test** from **11:00 AM to 12:00 PM**, which helped gauge our problem-solving abilities and readiness for the upcoming engineering coursework.

After a one-hour lunch break, we assembled in the **college auditorium** for an informative session on **scholarships**, where we learned about various financial aid opportunities available to deserving students.

The highlight of the afternoon was an engaging lecture by **Dr. Arashdeep Singh** on *“Food and Its Vital Role in a Student’s Overall Wellness”*. He explained the importance of a balanced diet, healthy eating habits, and nutrition for maintaining both physical and mental health.

Later, members of the **Causmic Club** addressed the students, motivating and guiding us towards positive thinking, self-awareness, and overall personality development.

---

## **Day 3 – Science, Happiness & Well-being**

The third day began at **9:00 AM** with a highly engaging session by **Dr. Jaswinder Singh**, whose humorous approach, live experiments, and interactive style kept everyone involved.  
His lecture on *“Meditation and Introspection in Daily Life”* helped us understand the importance of self-reflection and mindfulness for academic success and personal growth.

This was followed by an inspiring lecture by **Dr. Priya Darshini** on *“Achieving a State of Continuous Happiness and Prosperity”*. She emphasized the need to balance personal ambitions with ethical values, and to nurture both mental peace and career goals.

---

## **Day 4 – Peer Learning & Value Education**

The fourth day started at **9:30 AM** with **Peer-to-Peer (P2P) lectures** conducted by enthusiastic third-year seniors. These sessions provided us with valuable tips on effective study habits and a brief introduction to **C++ programming**, which was both insightful and motivating.

We then attended an **English lecture**, where the professor interacted with the entire class to understand our communication levels and expectations from the course.

Later in the day, we gathered in the **auditorium** for another **Causmic Club** interaction, followed by a thought-provoking lecture from **Dr. Priya Darshini** on *“Universal Human Values”*. She discussed the importance of empathy, cooperation, and ethical living in today’s world.

The day concluded with a **guidance session for students on purchasing laptops** best suited for engineering studies, covering specifications, budget considerations, and software requirements.
## **Day 5 – Academic Sessions & ID Formation**

The fifth day of the induction program began with a **BEEE (Basic Electrical and Electronics Engineering)** lecture from **Prof. Harminder Kaur** between **10:30 AM to 11:30 AM**. She introduced us to the fundamentals of electrical engineering and explained how these concepts will form the base for future courses.

This was followed by a **Chemistry lecture** by **Prof. Mandeep Kaur**, where she discussed the syllabus, shared important study tips, and guided us on how to prepare effectively for the subject.

After a 1.5-hour break, all students proceeded for the **college ID card formation process**, completing the necessary formalities for official identification.

---

## **Day 6 – College Insights & Motivation**

The day started with another **Chemistry lecture** by **Prof. Mandeep Kaur**, who shared more details about the college facilities and academic expectations.

Next, **Prof. Gagandeep Kaur** conducted a **Mathematics lecture**, introducing key concepts and giving us an overview of the semester topics.

Following a short break, all students gathered in the **auditorium** for a special interaction with **SR. Gurcharan Singh Grewal**, trustee of the Nankana Sahib Education Trust and an alumnus of GNDEC from the 19s. He warmly shared his college memories, asked students about their experience with hostel facilities, and encouraged open discussion. His friendly nature made the session interactive and inspiring.

Later, **Dr. Lakhver Singh**, Khanna Incharge, delivered an informative lecture on **NPTEL courses** and their benefits for self-paced learning. He also provided details about the various **college clubs and societies** that students could join to enhance their skills and interests.

---

## **Day 7 – Career Awareness & Peer Learning**

The seventh day began in the **auditorium** with a session by **Dr. Amit Kamra** on *Digital Marketing*. He explained the basics of online marketing, career opportunities in the field, and the growing importance of digital skills.

Next, **Dr. Amrinder Singh** introduced the **Welfare Awareness Committees and Societies** available in the college, highlighting their role in student development and support.

**Dr. Randhir Singh** then gave important information about the **examination process**, marking scheme, and tips for effective preparation.

Following this, **Prof. Harminder Kaur** conducted another **BEEE lecture**, this time focusing on **DC circuits** and their applications.

The day concluded with a **Peer-to-Peer session** where senior students continued guiding us in **C++ programming**, offering practical tips and resources to strengthen our coding skills.

---

## **Overall Experience**

The seven-day Induction Program at Guru Nanak Dev Engineering College was a truly enriching experience. Each day offered a blend of academic sessions, skill assessments, motivational talks, and interactive activities that helped us transition smoothly into college life.

We not only learned about our subjects and syllabus but also gained valuable guidance on personal development, career opportunities, wellness, and ethical values. The interactions with faculty, alumni, and senior students made us feel welcomed and motivated to strive for excellence.

Overall, the program laid a strong foundation for our journey ahead, encouraging us to make the best use of our time, resources, and opportunities at GNDEC.
